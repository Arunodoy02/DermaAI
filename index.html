Understanding Skin Cancer

Learn about different types and warning signs

Melanoma

The most dangerous type of skin cancer. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.

  • Asymmetry in shape
  • Border irregularity
  • Color variation
  • Diameter > 6mm
  • Evolving over time

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Most common and least dangerous. Appears as pearly or waxy bumps.

  • Slow-growing
  • Rarely spreads
  • Highly treatable
  • Common on sun-exposed areas

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Second most common. Appears as firm, red nodules or flat lesions.

  • Can spread if untreated
  • Scaly, crusted surface
  • Often on sun-damaged skin
  • Treatable when caught early

Benign Lesions

Non-cancerous skin growths that are typically harmless.

  • Moles (nevi)
  • Seborrheic keratoses
  • Skin tags
  • Still monitor for changes
